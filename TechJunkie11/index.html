About Me

It was a beautiful autumn day in the year of 1991. My mother was 8.5 months pregnant with her second child and was ready to pop! Going to the doctors, because she felt that she had to be ready to deliver considering her size, they told her to expect the baby around the middle of November. That night she went home shaking her head, ready and anxious to meet her new son. Sparing all other details, she gave birth to me, Antonino Colletti, on October 17th early in the morning.

English was not my first language growing up. Being that my parents are both Sicilian and my father was born in Venezuela, South America I've had some culture added to my life because of them. My Sicilian language, I believe, is better than my Spanish.

As far as religious views go, I am one of Jehovah's Witnesses. I do regularly go door to door and preach like you have probably known us to do. Being one of Jehovah's Witnesses does give me certain advantages. As one of my classes in school now is Presentations with Powerpoint, we must get in front of our peers and give a speach. Since I regularly do this at our Kingdom Hall(place of worship) in front of 60+ people this gives me an advantage.

Inspirations

My Biggest Inspirations

Growing up the man here in the picture has given me the qualities of life that many people strive to have. This is my grandfather on my mothers side, Mr. Antonino Fiorello, my biggest inspiration. I was named after my grandfather and it's one of the things I'm most proud about.

Never allow anyone to say you can't do something that you know you can do. Don't let them push you around because after the first time, they will keep pushing you. Be your own man and protect yourself and your family.

- Antonino Fiorello

He passed 2 years ago from liver cancer 1 week before his 73 birthday. I was able to spend one month with him before he started to get really bad. I watched the strongest man in my life slowly wither away. The thing that amazed me was that even though he was in so much pain, he always was thinking of his daughters and grand children. When I arrived at the hospital the first day I got there he started to cry cause I was there. He then proceeded to take the food off his hospital tray and try to feed me cause we had just gotten off the plane. Even in his worst times he would give his food up.

My grandfather was the best man I know. Anyone that has ever met him will tell you til this day that he was the most generous man. You never messed with the family though, that will get you in trouble with an Italian. Everyone said he was the most humble man, he loved his family first and then himself.
